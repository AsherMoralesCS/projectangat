About Us

History

The story of how ANGAT came to be and where we are headed.

ANGAT was conceptualized on March 23, 2026, during an initial brainstorming session focused on addressing poverty through education, empowerment, and community-driven action. From this point, the organization developed through structured planning, role assignment, and progressive digital creation, ultimately becoming a fully realized student-led initiative.

March 23, 2026

Conception of ANGAT

On March 23, 2026, project ANGAT was first conceptualized during a brainstorming session where the core mission of a poverty-alleviation organization centered on education and empowerment was established.

April 10, 2026

Foundation Building and Project Launch

On April 10, 2026, organizational roles were officially assigned, the initial website development began, and the project logo was designed, marking the start of ANGAT’s structured development phase.

April 11, 2026

Structural Development Completion

On April 11, 2026, Byron Leonard Alar submitted the completed Organizational Structure, defining the internal framework and departmental organization of ANGAT.

April 11, 2026

Vision and Direction Establishment

On April 11, 2026, Acel Lea Ardeño and Kath Leah Talbos submitted the Mission, Vision, Goals, and Objectives, solidifying the organization’s direction and long-term purpose.

April 11, 2026

Core Values Integration

On April 11, 2026, Adrienne Alexey Aguirre, Lyca Joyce Amido, and Marvie Lance Domingo submitted the Doctrines, Teachings, and Core Values, establishing the guiding principles and ethical foundation of ANGAT.

April 11, 2026

Creative Development Phase

On the evening of April 11, 2026, the development team (Asher) continued coding the website and created the supporting graphics and visual design elements to enhance structure and presentation.

April 12, 2026

Project Completion and Finalization

On April 12, 2026, the ANGAT website was completed, with all sections integrated and finalized, marking the successful culmination of planning, collaboration, and development.
